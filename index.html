Because that's what perseverance actually looks like, not some highlight reel, but the real, messy, human effort behind the screen.

When I'm not working or studying, I sing. It clears my head and reminds me to breathe. I also read (a lot). Mostly about people, systems, and how we all try to make sense of the chaos. Those habits shape how I lead: I try to stay curious, stay grounded, and remember that behind every process is a person.

My Life Philosophy

üåø Living by Connection

We can't do this alone, and we're not supposed to. I believe in community, not just the idea of it but the practice of it. If we want a village, we have to be the village - at work, at home, and everywhere in between.

That means showing up for your team when they're struggling. It means asking for help when you need it. It means celebrating someone else's win like it's your own. It means creating space for others to be human, too.

No one gets through life on their own strength. We all need people who see us, support us, and remind us we're not alone in the hard parts. I try to be that person whenever I can, because the support I've received has shaped who I am today.

At the end of the day, we're all just people trying to figure it out. The best thing we can do is figure it out together.
